The Story So Far

About Oscar

From indoor karting in South West London to the BRSCC Fiesta Junior grid — and aiming much further.

Roots

South West London Born & Raised

Oscar grew up in South West London, where a first taste of indoor karting turned into an obsession — and then a career plan. Every step since has been about getting faster, racing harder and climbing the motorsport ladder, from rental karts to a full national championship campaign in the #555 Fiesta.

The Journey

Karting to the Fiesta Junior Grid

The Beginning

TeamSport Indoor Karting

Where it all started — cutting laps on TeamSport's indoor circuits around London, learning racecraft, race lines and how to overtake in traffic.

Moving Up

Daytona Sandown Park — SuperChamps & Thursday Night League

Stepping up to outdoor competition at Daytona Sandown Park, racing in the SuperChamps series and the Thursday Night League — regular wheel-to-wheel racing against strong grids, week in, week out.

Serious Karting

Club 100

Graduating to Club 100, the UK's premier two-stroke arrive-and-drive karting championship — faster machinery, tighter margins and a proving ground that has launched plenty of professional careers.

2024

Junior ARDS Licence

Passed the Junior ARDS test in 2024, earning the racing licence that opens the door from karting into car racing.

Late 2024 – Early 2025

First Laps in the Fiesta

Testing the Mk7 Ford Fiesta through the end of 2024 and the start of 2025 — adapting from karts to tin-tops, learning slicks, braking points and race starts in a proper race car.

2025

BRSCC Fiesta Junior — Rookie Season

A full debut season in the BRSCC Fiesta Junior Championship in the #555 car, racing at circuits across the UK and building experience against the country's best junior tin-top drivers.

2026 — Now

Fiesta Junior, Year Two

Back on the 2026 grid with Race Car Consultants, pushing further up the order — with GB4 single-seaters and GT racing across Europe as the next targets on the ladder.
